## 2. Assessment of current links

### What is working well

**Anchor text quality is strong overall.** Links 1, 5, 6, and 7 all use descriptive, invitation-style anchor text that fits CoolMaps' brand guidelines. "Design their personalised map print" is a natural, conversion-oriented phrase that does not feel forced.

**The housewarming gift page is linked twice** (links 1 and 6), which is allowed under the guidelines but sits at the limit. The two anchor texts vary appropriately — "housewarming map print" vs "personalised housewarming gift" — which is good practice.

**The customiser (/create) is linked twice** (links 5 and 7), also with varied anchor text. Both placements feel genuinely contextual rather than bolted on.

**The three style links (Paper, Mineral, Modern) are well-placed** within the style showcase section. They appear in a naturally list-like context where readers would reasonably want to explore a specific style further.

**Distribution is reasonable but slightly uneven.** Five of the seven link instances fall within a single section ("The gift that brings home with them"), creating a cluster. The sections "Sentimental gifts that go beyond the practical", "Practical gifts worth giving", and the opening section contain no internal links at all.

### Issues to flag

**Duplicate page links eat into the 6-link maximum.** With 7 link instances across 5 URLs, the article has 2 duplicate destinations (/gifts/housewarming and /create each appear twice). Under the internal links map guidelines, the same page should not be linked more than once per article. This brings the article to 7 instances — just over the 6-link maximum stated in the SEO guidelines. One instance of each duplicate should be reviewed.

**The /create CTA at the very end (link 7) is a standalone paragraph, not embedded in body prose.** Guidelines specify that links placed within body paragraphs carry the most SEO value. The closing CTA line is structurally isolated and reads more like a footer prompt. It is still useful for conversion, but it contributes less SEO weight than an in-prose link would.

**Style links 2–4 anchor text is minimal.** "Paper style", "Mineral style", and "Modern style" are accurate and clear, but they are shorter and less descriptive than the guidelines' examples ("the Paper style", "vintage map print", "aged-paper map art"). They are not generic in the way "click here" is, but they could be slightly richer.

## 3. Opportunities to add or improve internal links

### Opportunity 1 — Add a link to the All Styles overview page in place of one /create duplicate

**Recommendation**: Replace the standalone closing CTA link to /create (link 7) with a link to https://coolmaps.app/styles — the All Styles overview page.

**Rationale**: The article introduces three styles (Paper, Mineral, Modern) but mentions eight exist. A reader who wants to compare all options before deciding has no path to that page. The All Styles page is a Tier 2 priority target and a natural next step for a reader who is interested but not yet certain which style suits the recipient.

**Where to insert**: The existing final paragraph reads:

> [Design their personalised map print](https://coolmaps.app/create), any location in the world, in eight distinct styles, with space for a date or a message.

The phrase "eight distinct styles" is the exact right hook. Revise to:

> [Browse all eight map styles](https://coolmaps.app/styles) or [design their map](https://coolmaps.app/create) directly — any location in the world, with space for a date or a message.

This preserves the CTA to /create, adds the styles overview link where it naturally belongs, and removes the duplicate /create instance (link 5 already covers the customiser mid-article).

**Net effect**: Resolves the over-limit issue, adds a Tier 2 destination, and better serves readers who want to compare styles before committing.

---

### Opportunity 2 — Add a sizing guide link in the "How to choose" section

**Recommendation**: Add a link to https://coolmaps.app/sizing-guide within the "How to choose: a simple framework" section.

**Rationale**: The section talks about a map going "on the wall of a new apartment abroad" and the James story references a framed map arriving from a friend. A reader who is close to purchasing will naturally wonder what size to order for a typical apartment wall. The sizing guide is a Tier 3 priority target and is specifically indicated for "any article discussing print sizes or wall art dimensions" — this article discusses framing and wall placement directly.

**Where to insert**: After the James story, the current text reads:

> If you know what place matters most to the person you're buying for, that's almost always where to start. The city they grew up in. The neighbourhood they're already saying they'll go back to one day. The town that made them who they are.

Append a brief sentence to the paragraph that follows the James story:

> Not sure which size works best for a new home? [Our sizing guide](https://coolmaps.app/sizing-guide) covers the most common wall dimensions and how different print sizes sit in a room.

This reads as a practical aside in a practical decision-making section, not a forced addition.

**Net effect**: Adds one Tier 3 destination with a natural hook, serves a genuine reader question at the decision-making stage of the article.
